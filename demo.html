The walkthrough

What a demo covers

A single patient journey, followed across every persona who touches it. This is roughly how a live session runs: one continuous story, with each role picking up where the last left off.

A patient is admitted, with no one typing it in

An existing hospital system fires an ADT A01 admit over HL7 v2. Greyquill ingests the message, parses the MSH and PID segments, and the patient appears on the floor automatically. No double entry, no custom integration. This is the coexistence layer at work, and it is where most demos begin. See the interoperability detail.

The doctor reviews the chart and places orders

The doctor opens the patient panel, reads the chart summary, and enters orders and an e-prescription. As the prescription is written, clinical decision support surfaces a drug and allergy safety alert before it is signed, so the risk is caught at the point of decision rather than after.

The nurse runs the medication pass

The nurse records vitals, works the eMAR to administer the ordered medications, and adds a nursing note. At shift change, the nurse files a structured SBAR handover so the next shift inherits the full picture, not a hallway summary.

Pharmacy, lab, and imaging pick up the orders

The order flows downstream. Pharmacy works its dispense queue against live inventory. The lab tech enters results and tracks the specimen. The radiographer works a modality worklist and captures images with a QC step. Each role sees only its own queue, already populated.

Billing works the money

The biller opens the AR worklist, reviews the invoice, posts a payment, and clears a denial through the follow-up queue, then reconciles it on the payments ledger. The clinical visit and the revenue cycle are the same system, not two databases stitched together.

The operational breadth most platforms skip

Beyond the bedside: facilities and biomed work the asset register, work orders, and equipment calibration; the EMS crew works the ambulance status board, dispatch, and completed trip history; the clinic coordinator runs outpatient clinics and day reports; and HR works the credentialing vault, tracking license and credential expiry. A hospital is more than its wards, and so is this.

The compliance officer sees everything that just happened

The compliance officer opens the audit trail and finds every write from the journey above, each tagged with the acting user and, where relevant, the patient. Reads are not logged, only changes to state. To close the loop, the same patient's full clinical record exports as a FHIR $everything Bundle, demonstrating the data is portable, not locked in.

Access

How access works

The live environment is access-controlled. Greyquill provisions a time-boxed demo login for your hospital when you request one, which is why there are deliberately no credentials or URLs on this public page. We hand them to your reviewers directly, scoped to the people who need them.

  • Synthetic data only. The demo runs on seeded, sample data. No real patients, no real records, nothing sensitive ever touches the demo build.
  • One login, every role. You explore the full set of personas through a single unified staff login, with the patient app alongside it.
  • Multi-tenant by design. Your demo tenant is isolated at the data layer, so what you see is yours and only yours.
  • Time-boxed. Access is granted for the evaluation window and retired afterward, with no lingering credentials.

This is a pilot build with seeded sample data. We will be candid in the session about what is fully shipped and what is phased on the roadmap, so you can plan against reality.
