A phased rollout

From "running alongside" to "system of record," step by step

A progressive path where every stage is reversible and nothing forces a cut-over. Each step leans on a real, shipped capability, not a promise.

Connect & observe

Stand Greyquill up in hard multi-tenant isolation on infrastructure you control (native deploy, no Docker: Fastify API under pm2/systemd, Postgres, behind your reverse proxy). Point your existing ADT feed at the HL7 v2 ingest endpoint POST /hl7/v2/ingest: admit (A01), discharge (A03) and update (A08) events parse from MSH/PID segments and mirror patient state into Greyquill automatically. Nothing changes for existing staff, you are simply observing the platform stay in sync.

Pilot one department

Go live with a single team, a ward, the pharmacy dispense queue, or the AR/billing worklist, while everything else stays on your incumbent systems. Orders and results flow back out to your LIS/RIS as HL7 v2 via GET /hl7/v2/orders/:serviceRequestId/message (ORM/ORU), and the same patient data is readable over FHIR R4 (/fhir/Patient, /fhir/Observation, /fhir/Condition). The pilot runs in parallel with no impact on the rest of the hospital.

Expand role by role

As confidence grows, switch on more of the 13 staff roles, nursing and eMAR, labs, imaging, facilities and biomedical, EMS/ambulance, outpatient clinics, HR/credentialing, compliance. One unified staff login already covers every role; permissions come from the account, so adding a team is a configuration step, not a new system. Patients can come online with the passwordless phone + OTP patient app when you are ready.

Become system of record

Where Greyquill has proven out, make it primary for that domain. Keep FHIR and HL7 v2 bridges to anything you choose to retain, and use the FHIR $everything export to move complete patient records on your terms. No big-bang, no forced cut-over: you advance domain by domain, and you can stop or hold at any stage.

Every blocker, answered

The real reasons hospitals hesitate, and how Greyquill handles each

Grounded in what the platform actually does today. Where something is roadmap, we say so plainly below.

ConcernHow Greyquill handles it
"We can't rip out our current HIS / LIS / RIS / PACS." You don't. Greyquill coexists via HL7 v2 and FHIR R4: it runs alongside your incumbents and exchanges data with them. Inbound ADT drives patient state via POST /hl7/v2/ingest; orders and results flow back out as HL7 v2 ORM/ORU. Your existing systems keep operating.
"Data migration is too risky." Migration is incremental, never big-bang. ADT ingest mirrors patients automatically, and you migrate one department at a time. Because the full patient record is exportable via FHIR GET /fhir/Patient/:id/$everything, your data is never trapped, you can pull a complete record out at any point. No lock-in, by design.
"Downtime or disruption to patient care." The rollout is phased and department-by-department, and the pilot runs in parallel with your live systems, so a single team going live does not touch the rest of the hospital. Deployment is native on your own infrastructure, and every deploy runs a per-persona smoke check (each role's worklist plus a cross-role 403 check) as an automated go/no-go gate.
"Staff training and change fatigue." It is one consistent app (web + mobile from a single Flutter codebase) with role-scoped views: staff only ever see their own workflow. You roll out to one team at a time, so training is focused and incremental rather than a hospital-wide relearning event.
"Security and patient-data compliance." Role-Based Access Control with ~28 granular permissions, deny-by-default: every endpoint is guarded and a missing permission returns HTTP 403. Hard multi-tenant isolation (every query is scoped to the caller's tenant at the data layer, not just the UI). A complete audit trail captures every successful staff write, with the acting user, the patient, and the action. See Platform & Security →
"Vendor lock-in." Open standards in and out. FHIR R4 and HL7 v2 on both directions, plus full-record export via FHIR $everything. Your data stays portable and your interoperability is standards-native, so you are never structurally locked to Greyquill.
"Cost and unclear ROI." Start small, one department, to prove value before you scale spend. Consolidating point systems under one platform (clinical, RCM, pharmacy, labs, imaging, facilities, EMS, HR, compliance) and automating handoffs reduces duplicated tooling and manual reconciliation across teams. You expand only where it has already paid off.
"Integration effort on our IT team." The connectors are standards-based, so wiring an existing ADT feed in means little-to-no custom code: it is an HL7 v2 message stream into one endpoint. Greyquill Software delivers and operates the platform with you, so the heavy lifting does not land on your IT staff.

⇄ Standards-native interoperability

FHIR R4 and HL7 v2 are first-class, in and out. Adopting Greyquill future-proofs your data exchange rather than adding one more proprietary silo to integrate around.

An honest note: this is a pilot-stage platform

Greyquill Health Platform is demonstrably feature-complete across the modules above and runs live, but it is a demo / pilot build with seeded sample data. It is not yet certified (no HIPAA / ABDM / NABH / ISO claims), and some integrations are on the roadmap: real SSO, production patient-auth hardening, DICOM/PACS image transport, and the video-consult and ambient-scribe interfaces. We would harden these together with you during a pilot, scoped to your environment. We present this as a partnership: prove the platform on one department, then expand and certify alongside your real workflows.
