Clinical care

At the bedside and in the consult room

The clinical core: how doctors run their day, place orders, prescribe, document, and get decision support inline.

🩺 Doctor

Login persona: Doctor

The treating clinician's command center, from the daily schedule to the chart to order entry, with decision support and safety checks surfaced where care is decided.

  • Patient panel — the doctor's roster of patients under their care.
  • Daily schedule — the day's appointments and rounds at a glance.
  • Chart summary — a consolidated view of the patient's record.
  • Clinical notes — structured documentation of the encounter.
  • Order entry — place lab, imaging, and other service orders.
  • Orders & results — track placed orders and review returned results.
  • e-Prescribe — issue medication orders that flow to pharmacy.
  • Clinical decision support — risk scores plus drug and allergy safety alerts.
  • Telemed — remote consult worklist (see roadmap note below on video).

🛡 Clinical decision support

Surfaced inline, where care is decided

CDS is not a separate destination. It rides along with the clinical workflow so the right prompt appears at the moment of the decision, not after.

What CDS means here: two things, surfaced inline. Risk scores computed from the patient's data, and drug / allergy safety alerts raised when an order or prescription could cause harm. They appear within order entry, e-prescribe, and the chart rather than in a report you have to go find.

The same coded clinical data behind these checks is what the platform also exposes over standards-based APIs. See Interoperability for how problems, allergies, medications, and observations leave the building as FHIR resources.

Revenue cycle

From charge to cash

The billing desk's full accounts-receivable workflow, including the parts that actually recover money: denials and reconciliation.

💵 Biller

Login persona: Biller

A complete revenue-cycle management worklist, not just invoice printing: the path from open receivable to posted payment to chased denial.

  • AR worklist — outstanding accounts receivable to work.
  • Invoice detail — line-level view of an individual invoice.
  • Payment posting — record payments against invoices.
  • Denials follow-up queue — work rejected and denied claims.
  • Payments reconciliation ledger — match payments to expected amounts.

📊 Why this matters

Revenue leaks at the seams: denials that nobody chases, payments that never get reconciled. Greyquill puts those steps in the same worklist as the invoice, so the recovery work has an owner and a queue.

  • Denials have a dedicated follow-up queue, not a spreadsheet.
  • Reconciliation is a ledger, so a payment either matches or it surfaces.
  • The admin KPI rollup includes AR invoices, so leadership sees the same numbers.

Facilities & biomedical

The building and the equipment in it

The infrastructure that keeps clinical care running: assets, work orders, preventive maintenance, and the calibration of medical devices.

🔧 Facilities Tech

Login persona: Facilities Tech

A full facilities and biomedical engineering workflow, including the regulated part: keeping clinical equipment calibrated and in service.

  • Asset register — the inventory of physical assets.
  • Work-order queue — maintenance jobs to action.
  • Preventive-maintenance (PM) schedule — planned servicing before things break.
  • Biomedical equipment service — calibration tracking for medical devices.

⚡ Why biomedical lives here

Clinical equipment is a patient-safety asset. Tracking calibration and PM in the same system as the work-order queue means an out-of-date device shows up before it is used, not after.

  • Calibration tracking ties each device to its service history.
  • PM schedules drive work orders proactively.

Governance & compliance

Who changed state, on the record

A tamper-evident audit trail and the role that reads it.

🔎 Compliance Officer

Login persona: Compliance Officer

Read-only oversight of the platform's audit trail, the system of record for what staff did.

  • Audit / compliance log — read-only view over the audit trail.

📝 How the audit trail works

Every successful staff write is captured automatically: a non-blocking hook records the acting user, the patient (when patient-scoped), and the action, after the response is already sent.

Design principle: "who changed state, not who looked." Reads are not logged, so the trail is signal, not noise. The hook runs after the response and swallows its own errors, so auditing can never slow down or break a clinical action.

Multi-tenant isolation and RBAC are enforced at the data layer, not just hidden in the UI. See Platform & Security for the full model.

Patient experience

The patient's own app

A dedicated patient application, separate from staff, with its own passwordless sign-in.

Login: phone number + OTP (passwordless)

Everything the patient needs to engage with their own care, on web or mobile from the same codebase.

  • Appointments — book and view visits.
  • Payments — settle what they owe.
  • Medical record — their own clinical record.

Phased: the video consult itself and the ambient scribe are interface stubs at this stage, on the roadmap rather than shipped. Patient-auth hardening and real SSO are also phased. We present these honestly, not as done.
